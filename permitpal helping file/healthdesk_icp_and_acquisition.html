These profiles look tempting but will drain your time, generate support tickets, and churn early. Avoid them in Month 1–6.
Large hospital-affiliated clinics (10+ practitioners)
They need procurement sign-off, IT security reviews, and integration with enterprise EMRs. Sales cycles are 6–12 months. You'll spend months on calls and get nothing. Target them in Year 2 with a Group+ enterprise tier.
Practitioners who "just need a simple calendar"
If they're not billing Medicare or NDIS and have fewer than 10 patients/week, HealthDesk is overkill. They'll complain the product is complex and churn in 30 days. Calendly or HotDoc is the right fit for them.
Practices in financial stress or closing
Any clinic complaining "we're losing money" will never pay full price. They'll negotiate hard, pay late, and churn at the first billing cycle. Their complaints will also distort your product feedback signal.
Practitioners who refuse to switch from paper
Older clinicians (55+) who still use paper notes and fax referrals will require enormous support investment. You'll be teaching them to use a computer, not your product. The ROI is negative for both of you in early stage.
Non-AHPRA disciplines (e.g. massage, naturopathy)
HealthDesk's core moat is Medicare/NDIS billing which requires AHPRA registration. Massage therapists and naturopaths can't access these schemes, making your key differentiator worthless to them.
An exact 8-week sequence. Not "find people online" — specific actions, in order, with what to say.
1
Week 1 — Build your target list of 50 clinics
Search Google Maps for "physiotherapy clinic Sydney", "occupational therapist Melbourne", "speech pathology Brisbane". Filter to 1–5 star clinics with Google reviews mentioning "NDIS" or "Medicare". Export name, phone, website, Google Maps URL into a spreadsheet. Aim for 50 names before doing any outreach.
Tool: Phantombuster Google Maps scraper or do it manually. Takes ~3 hours.
2
Week 1 — Find the owner's name on LinkedIn
Search LinkedIn for "physiotherapist + clinic name + Sydney". Most clinic owners have LinkedIn profiles. Note: sole traders often ARE the practitioner. If you find a practice manager, even better — they're the real decision maker for software.
Search: "physiotherapy practice owner Melbourne" — filter by 2nd-degree connections first.
3
Week 2 — Join and listen in Facebook groups for 1 week
Join: "Australian Physiotherapy Network", "Australian OT Network", "NDIS Allied Health Providers Australia", "Speech Pathology Australia Practitioners". Don't post yet. Read the complaints. Screenshot the ones about Medicare billing, Nookal, Cliniko, NDIS paperwork. These are your exact pain points to mirror back.
Typical complaints: "Cliniko doesn't do bulk-bill claims", "NDIS invoicing is killing me", "Anyone found a better option than Nookal?"
4
Week 2 — Send 20 warm LinkedIn messages (not cold)
Comment genuinely on 3 of their posts first. Then send a connection request with a personalised note (see Outreach tab). Message is NOT a sales pitch — it's a research request. "I'm building something for physios dealing with Medicare bulk-bill headaches. Can I get 20 minutes of your perspective?" Response rate: 15–25% with this approach.
Personalise every message. Mention their clinic name and one specific thing from their profile. Generic messages get 2% response rate.
5
Week 3 — Run 10 discovery calls (not demos)
Don't show the product yet. Ask: "Walk me through how you handle Medicare claims today. What software do you use? What do you hate about it? If you could wave a magic wand, what would billing look like?" Listen for the exact words they use — use those words back in your copy. Offer a $50 Uber Eats voucher for their time if needed.
The goal is intel, not a sale. Every sentence they say is a marketing headline you didn't have to write.
6
Week 4 — Offer 5 beta spots (free for 3 months)
From your 10 discovery calls, pick the 5 whose pain matches your MVP best. Offer: "I'd love to have you as a founding clinic — 3 months free, you get the product first, I get 30 min per week of your feedback." Frame it as an exclusive. Most will say yes because you've already built rapport over two calls.
Don't offer to everyone. Scarcity matters. "We're only taking 5 founding clinics" is true and creates urgency.
7
Week 5–6 — Weekly check-in calls with beta clinics
30 min every week. Ask: "What broke? What confused you? What did you wish it did?" Fix bugs within 24 hours. Add the features they ask for most — this is your roadmap validation. One happy beta clinic will spontaneously tell 3 colleagues. This is how you go from 5 to 10.
Send a Loom video after every fix: "You mentioned X was broken — I just fixed it, here's how it works now." This builds extraordinary loyalty.
8
Week 7–8 — Ask for referrals + post in Facebook groups
Ask each beta clinic: "Do you know 2–3 other physios or OTs who complain about Cliniko or NDIS billing?" Most will name someone. Also post a helpful answer in the Facebook groups (not a pitch): "I've been researching Medicare bulk-bill workflows for physios — here's what I found..." and mention the product naturally at the end. Convert 5 more clinics this way.
The Facebook group post must give genuine value first. A resource, a guide, a comparison table. The mention of HealthDesk is almost a footnote.
LinkedIn connection request (OT clinic owner with NDIS caseload)
Hi [Name], I noticed [Clinic Name] works with NDIS participants — I'm building practice management software that automates the NDIS PRODA billing that most OTs do manually. Would love to connect and get your perspective on what's broken in the current tools.
Keep under 300 chars for connection request field. Personalise [Clinic Name] every time.
Follow-up message after connecting (research ask, not a pitch)
Thanks for connecting, [Name]. I'm doing research interviews with allied health practitioners about practice management — specifically around Medicare and NDIS billing. No pitch, just 20 minutes of your time to understand the pain points. Would you be open to a quick call this week? Happy to share what I'm finding with other clinics too.
Research framing gets 3x more responses than "I'd love to show you my product."

I've spent the last 3 months interviewing physios and OTs about their Medicare billing workflows. A few things I found surprising: — Most clinics submitting bulk-bill claims still do it manually through PRODA, spending 3–5 hours/week — Only 2 of the 40+ clinics I spoke to had automated NDIS budget tracking — The most common complaint about Cliniko: no native Medicare Online integration Happy to share the full summary if useful. I'm building something to fix this — if you're one of the clinics drowning in NDIS paperwork and want early access, let me know.
Post this after 1 week of being active in the group. Pure value first. The "building something" line is the soft CTA.
Beta invite (after discovery call — closing the founding clinic)
Hi [Name], really appreciated your time last week — your point about NDIS CSV exports taking half a day every month stuck with me. I'd like to offer you one of our 5 founding clinic spots: 3 months completely free, and I'll build the NDIS features around your exact workflow. In return, 30 minutes per week of honest feedback. We're only taking 5 clinics at this stage so I can give each one proper attention. Interested?
Reference something specific from their discovery call. Shows you listened. Never send this without a prior conversation.
